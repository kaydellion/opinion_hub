2. Ratings

Description: Respondents rate a particular item, service, or experience on a scale (e.g., 1 to 5 stars, or 1 to 10).
    
Example:

        Question: ‚ÄúHow would you rate our customer service?‚Äù
        Scale: 1 (Very Poor) to 5 (Excellent)

3. Open-ended 

Description: Respondents provide a free-text answer without any predefined options, allowing for more detailed and qualitative feedback.
Example:

        Question: ‚ÄúWhat improvements would you like to see in our product?‚Äù

4. Word Cloud

Description: Respondents submit one or more words or short phrases in response to a question. The most frequent responses are displayed more prominently in a word cloud.

    Example:

        Question: ‚ÄúWhat word best describes your experience with our brand?‚Äù



5. Quiz

Description: A poll with correct and incorrect answers, designed to test the knowledge of the respondents. It‚Äôs often used for educational purposes or fun engagement.

Example:

        Question: ‚ÄúWhich planet is known as the Red Planet?‚Äù
        Options:

            Earth
            Mars
            Venus
            Jupiter

        Correct Answer: Mars

6. Assessment

Description: Similar to a quiz, but more focused on evaluating a person‚Äôs knowledge, skills, or attitudes. Often used in training or education to gauge understanding.

Example:

        Question: ‚ÄúHow confident are you in using the new project management software?‚Äù
        Options:

            Not Confident
            Slightly Confident
            Moderately Confident
            Very Confident

7. Yes/No 

Description: A binary poll where respondents choose between two options: Yes or No.
    
Example: ‚ÄúDo you agree with the new company policy?‚Äù

8. Multiple Answer 

Description: Respondents can select multiple answers, but each answer is treated individually rather than as a ranked choice.
    
Example: ‚ÄúWhich sports do you follow? (Select all that apply)‚Äù

9. Dichotomous 

Description: Similar to a Yes/No poll, but can include any two mutually exclusive options.

Example: ‚ÄúDo you prefer coffee or tea?‚Äù

10. Matrix 

Description: A grid-based poll where respondents evaluate multiple items across several dimensions or criteria.
    
Example: ‚ÄúRate the following products based on quality, price, and ease of use.‚Äù

11. Date

Description: Collects a specific date input from respondents.
Example:
Question: ‚ÄúWhat is your preferred date for the company event?‚Äù

1. Bar Chart

A bar chart uses rectangular bars to represent data values. The bars can be vertical or horizontal, and their lengths are proportional to the values they represent.

Example:

üîπ Satisfied ‚Äì 60%
üîπ Neutral ‚Äì 25%
üîπ Dissatisfied ‚Äì 15%

üîπ January ‚Äì 45%

A treemap chart displays hierarchical data as nested rectangles. The size of each rectangle represents a proportion.
